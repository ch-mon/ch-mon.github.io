About

My name is Chris (actually Christoph, but simply call me Chris) and I warmly welcome you on my website. If I had to summarize myself in one sentence, it would be the following: I am a character who is driven by a strong passion for learning & utilizing the newest technologies (it is crazy what is currently happening out there), while on the other hand trying to retain balance in the fast-paced environment of our today’s world. Luckily, I got more than a sentence, and thus I want to motivate this website and its structure with the following story.

In 2018, during my time at Toastmasters International in Cologne, I gave several speeches. One of them was about Curriculum Vitae (CVs) and my opinion about them. The main takeaway was: CVs are comparable to a mask. A mask that we put on when we are on the internet or in a job interview. Those masks distill on a high-level who we are. And there is nothing wrong with this. As long as we stay aware of the purpose of CVs.

If you visit my LinkedIn profile, you will probably see the following Alter-Ego of mine: Former Physics student at EKU Tuebingen; got involved in Consulting during studies & several internships; currently living in Switzerland and writing his Master’s thesis at ETH Zurich.

However, in my opinion, we often judge people to quickly based upon their mask, thinking that we know which label to attach to them. We never get the full story, those nitty-gritty turns & most shaping experiences that underlie those CV milestones. CVs reduce us to our most important stations and technical skills. Sometimes, there is a tiny section about your hobbies in the end. Nevertheless, I believe there is so much more to us that remains under the surface. That is why I decided to create this website. To give you a more rounded view onto who I think I am, what fascinates me, and what my interests are beyond work and university. I want to give you the chance to look behind my mask.

In my Blog, I write about past and present thoughts, experiences, and ideas. The Personal page illustrates what I am currently doing and lists some of my most shaping experiences of the last years. The Projects page summarizes some interesting challenges I worked on. Since I love reading, feel free to rummage through my Reads to see which books influence and have influenced me.
