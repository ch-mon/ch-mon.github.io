Reads

Here are some books, I managed to read over the years. It is crazy how fast it adds up and how the knowledge becomes larger than the sum of its parts by making connections. Currently, I am still trying to figure out when to re-read or re-listen to books, but as a good quote says

I cannot remember the books I’ve read any more than the meals I have eaten; even so, they have made me. -Ralph Waldo Emerson

Within each year, the titles listed above the line reflect the books I have read as a hardcover, the ones below the line I listened to in audiobook format.

2021

Factfulness

  • Overcoming biases in our thinking and seeing the world more like it actually is.

Barking up the wrong tree

  • What does science (on a high level) know about success in our private and professional lives.

A short history of time

  • What is time from a physicist’s point of view? Where does the universe come from and where is it probably heading?

Paradox (Fiction)

  • Thrilling story about development of humanity, extraterrestrial life, and the universe.

2020

The Physicists

  • A thought which has once put into the world, cannot be taken back anymore.

Doing good better

  • Aim for problems which are big (i.e. the number of people affected), important (what is the amplitude of the expected outcome), and neglected (are people already working on this).

Surely you are joking, Mr. Feynman

  • Be curious and integer about the work you do.

Origin (Fiction)

  • Is AI going to outsmart us without us noticing?

The Book of Why

  • High-level introduction on Causality I recommend to everybody working in science.

Homo Deus

  • Most of our striving is motivated by immortality, happiness, and god-likeness.

Real Help

  • Stop listening to self-help books. Knowledge is not going to advance you after a certain threshold - acting & practicing does.

The Happiness Hypothesis

  • Most of the time we are steered more by our subconscious thought patterns than our rational self. Social relationships, meaningful work, continuous learning, progressing towards a goal, companionate love, and being part of something larger than yourself tremendously influence our perceived happiness.

Inferno (Fiction)

  • Random sterilization against overpopulation?

21 lessons for the 21st century

  • AI might be able to understand our internal biological algorithms better than us humans and this information can easily be used for the good or bad.

The Kangaroo Chronicles (Fiction)

  • Putting the finger on political topics where it hurts.

Can’t hurt me

  • We can endure a lot more than we think we are capable.

Antidote

  • We underestimate how inflation and interest are going to impact us in the long run.

Indistractable

  • We cannot call something a distraction unless we know what it is distracting us from.

Ultralearning

  • Often, we prefer passive knowledge consumption over active practice, even though we benefit the most from the latter.

2019

The Nutrition Compass

  • Eating some crisps & sweets now and then? Perfectly alright. Eating them every single day? You bear the consequences.

Vagabonding

  • Vagabonding as a mindset rather than a one-time trip.

Digital Minimalism

  • Use technology to your advantage and be not used by it.

The decision is yours

  • Not making a decision is also a decision which has consequences.

Success is what follows

  • Passion is the product of intense work, not the other way around.

Silicon Germany

  • Digitalization is a continuous process of continuous reinvention and should not be seen as a one-time project.

The Power of Now

  • Too often we ruminate about the things that could have been or will be, instead of focusing on the one real thing that we actually have: the present moment.

Atomic Habits

  • We do not rise to the levels of our ambitions, but fall to the levels of our systems. Focus on your processes rather than goals.

The Power of Habit

  • Be aware of the Cue-Response-Reward cycle in your daily life.

The Secret

  • A bit too esoteric, but it generally certainly helps if you know what you want.

Winning

  • Integrity, intelligence, maturity, optimism, authenticity, and heavy-duty resilience are crucial values for the workforce of a company.

2018

Tools of Titans

  • Meditate, exercise, be eager to learn, and dare to try new things every day.

Unbox your life

  • You become the average of the people around you. Thus, choose your environment wisely.

1984

  • Language acts as a glass ceiling to the thoughts we can have.

10% Happier

  • Meditation as a path to inner peace.

Richer than the Geissens

  • If you spend one more dollar than you earn, you will go broke - it’s all mathematics.

The richest man in Babylon

  • Thy shall invest 10% of your income.

The Millionaire’s Fastlane

  • Entrepreneurship as the fast lane versus investing as the slow lane.

Never eat alone

  • Do not be one of those networking schmoozers but instead show genuine interest and listen to other people.

The subtle art of not giving a fck

  • If there is no reason to do anything, there is also no reason not to do something.

2017

How to win Friends

  • A stranger is just a friend you have not met yet.

The laws of winning

  • The only thing that matters happens today. Tomorrow will always be in the future.

Investment Punk

  • Financial dullness is expensive.

The four-hour workweek

  • Doing something unimportant well does not make it important. Requiring a lot of time for a task does not make it relevant.
